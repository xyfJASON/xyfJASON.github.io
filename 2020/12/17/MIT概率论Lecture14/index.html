Lecture 14: Introduction to Bayessian inference

Overview

  • The big picture
    • motivation, applications
    • problem types (hypothesis testing, estimation, etc.)
  • Theh general framework
    • Bayes’ rule $\rightarrow$ posterior (4 versions)
    • point estimates (MAP, LMS)
    • performance measures (prob. of error; mean squared error)
    • examples

The big picture

The Bayesian inference framework

  • Unknown $\Theta$
    • treated as a r.v.
    • prior distribution $p_\Theta$ or $f_\Theta$
  • Observation $X$
    • observation model $p_{X\mid\Theta}$ or $f_{X\mid\Theta}$
  • Use appropriate version of the Bayes rule to find $p_{\Theta\mid X}(\bullet\mid X=x)$ or $f_{\Theta\mid X}(\bullet\mid X=x)$.


Point estimates in Bayesian inference:

  • Maximum a posteriori probability (MAP)

  • Least Mean Squares (LMS)
